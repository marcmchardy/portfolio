Eggplant & Butterbean Ragu

Rich and full of flavour, the eggplant lends a caramelised fattiness to this dish while the combination of cherry tomato, fennel seed, and lemon zest lift it up to be fresh and satisfying.

Servings: 4
Time: 1 hour
  • 1 eggplant
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 can cherry (or diced) tomatoes
  • 1 diced onion
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 1 grated carrot
  • 1 tsp lemon rind
  • 1/2 cup of red wine
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable stock
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 3 anchovies
  1. Slice the eggplant into lengths, I like to do bits about 2cm by 2cm wide, and about 5cm long. Place these flat between paper towels, sprinkle with salt and leave for 10 minutes to remove excess moisture.
  2. Heat a pan on medium. When hot add half of the olive oil, add the eggplant and make sure it gets fully covered. Fry until the eggplant shows signs of browning, adding more oil if the pan goes dry.
  3. Add the diced onion, garlic, and lemon zest to the pan. Carefully stir, attempting to keep the eggplant together. Add more oil and a splash of the stock to keep the mixture from burning.
  4. Grind the peppercorns, fennel seeds, and salt in a morter and pestel. Add the spice mix, along with the paprika and diced anchovies to the pan, sauté for a minute before adding the can of tomatoes, wine, and stock.
  5. Add the bay leaves, stir, put a lid on and leave on a low heat for around 15–25 minutes.
  6. Drain the can of butter beans, rinse well, and add to the pan, stir and leave for another 15 minutes.
  7. Before serving, remove the bay leaves.
  8. Serve with freshly cooked pasta, or use as a base in a vegetarian lasagne.
